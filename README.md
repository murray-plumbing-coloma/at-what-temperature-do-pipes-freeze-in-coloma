## Why Pipes Freeze (What Changes the ‚ÄúFreezing Temperature‚Äù)

Even though water freezes at **32¬∞F**, pipes often freeze due to **heat loss** and **exposure** factors, including:

- **Pipe location:** exterior walls, attics, crawl spaces, garages  
- **Insulation level:** no insulation or missing vapor barrier  
- **Air leaks:** gaps around plumbing penetrations under sinks / at foundations  
- **Wind chill:** drafts in vented crawl spaces and open eaves  
- **Pipe material & size:** small-diameter lines freeze faster  
- **Water movement:** stagnant water freezes faster than a slow drip

## What To Do If Pipes Freeze (Safe Steps)

1. **Turn on a faucet** (both hot and cold) to relieve pressure.
2. **Check the obvious cold zones:** under sinks, garage, crawl space access, exterior walls.
3. **Warm the area safely:** space heater (attended), warm towels, or a hair dryer.
4. **Do NOT use open flames** (torch/lighter) near pipes.
5. **If you see bulging, cracks, or leaking:** shut off the main water and call a plumber.

## Prevention Checklist (Best Practices)

- Insulate exposed pipes (crawl space, garage, exterior walls)
- Seal air leaks around pipe penetrations
- Keep cabinet doors open during cold nights (under sinks)
- Maintain indoor heat (even when away)
- Let faucets drip during hard freezes (only if needed)
- Disconnect and drain outdoor hoses; shut off exterior bibs if possible
